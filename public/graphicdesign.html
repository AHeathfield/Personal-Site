Logo Challenge. Limited Geometry.

If you haven't noticed the trend yet most these projects were assignments in my comtech class. This one was making a Logo for any company we want (even a made up one) and we can only use 3 cirlces, rectangles, and triangles. If you get technical this one breaks that rule but when I made it in Illustrator I actually only created 3 of each shape (triangle 2 I think). The made up company was for a future space company. There purpose was basically anaylzing data between space transportation, so time of arrival, cost for fuel, any other space data. They would also handle the currency exchange between planets.

Rotoscope. Yuji Nishida.

This project was done using a technique called rotoscoping. Basically you take a video you find online and you trace out the bodies of people and objects each frame. I did 12 frames per second so it's basically 60 drawings and I also wanted to add some cool effects by focusing on him and adding smoother movement by just drawing lines. At the time I was on the volleyball team and volleyball is one of my favourite sports which inspired me to make this.

GEOMATRIX. One Piece inspired.

This was done using rotoscoping as well, but with my own twist. The scene is from one of my favourite animes One Piece, But I didn't want to just copy the character's drawing. So I made them stickmen and added my own details. This was for the app I was making for my Co-op "Geomatrix" which is a competitive math game. I wanted some attack animation for the prototype, so I made this.
